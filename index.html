Introduction to Fractions

Welcome to our accessible learning module on fractions. This module is designed according to NCERT Class 4 Mathematics standards to help all students, including those with visual or hearing impairments, understand the concept of fractions.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what fractions are and how they represent parts of a whole
  • Identify different types of fractions
  • Compare fractions with the same denominator
  • Learn basic operations with fractions
  • Apply fractions to everyday situations

Understanding Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. When we divide something into equal parts, each part is a fraction of the whole.

1/4 (one-fourth) of the square is shaded

Parts of a Fraction

A fraction has two parts:

  • Numerator: The number on the top. It tells how many parts we are talking about.
  • Denominator: The number on the bottom. It tells how many equal parts the whole is divided into.
3 4

This is read as "three-fourths" or "three quarters"

In our daily life, we use fractions all the time:

  • When we cut a roti into 2 equal pieces, each piece is 1/2 (one-half) of the roti
  • When we share a chocolate bar with 3 friends equally, each one gets 1/4 (one-fourth) of the chocolate
  • Half an hour (30 minutes) is 1/2 of an hour

Types of Fractions

Proper Fractions

The numerator is less than the denominator.

2 5

These represent less than one whole thing.

Improper Fractions

The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

7 4

These represent one or more than one whole thing.

Mixed Fractions

Combination of a whole number and a proper fraction.

1
3 4

This represents one whole plus three-fourths.

Converting Between Types

Improper to Mixed Fraction:

  1. Divide the numerator by the denominator
  2. The quotient becomes the whole number part
  3. The remainder becomes the new numerator
  4. Keep the denominator the same

Example: 7/4 = 1 3/4 (7 ÷ 4 = 1 with remainder 3)

Mixed to Improper Fraction:

  1. Multiply the whole number by the denominator
  2. Add the numerator to this product
  3. Put this sum over the original denominator

Example: 1 3/4 = 7/4 (1 × 4 + 3 = 7, so 7/4)

Comparing Fractions

Same Denominators

When fractions have the same denominator, the one with the larger numerator is greater.

2 5
<
3 5

2/5 is less than 3/5 because 2 is less than 3

2/5 (two-fifths)

3/5 (three-fifths)

Equivalent Fractions

Different fractions can represent the same amount. These are called equivalent fractions.

1 2
=
2 4
=
3 6

To find equivalent fractions, multiply or divide both the numerator and denominator by the same number.

Operations with Fractions

Adding Fractions with Same Denominator

When adding fractions with the same denominator:

  1. Add the numerators
  2. Keep the denominator the same
  3. Simplify if needed

+
2 4
=
3 4
+
=

1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4

Adding Fractions with Different Denominators

When adding fractions with different denominators:

  1. Find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of the denominators
  2. Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with this common denominator
  3. Add the numerators and keep the common denominator
  4. Simplify if needed

Real-Life Examples

Fractions in Everyday Life

  • Food: When we divide a chapati into 4 equal parts, each part is 1/4 of the chapati
  • Time: 30 minutes is 1/2 of an hour, 15 minutes is 1/4 of an hour
  • Money: 50 paise is 1/2 of a rupee
  • Measurement: 500 ml is 1/2 of a litre

Word Problems

Problem 1:

Reena ate 1/4 of a pizza, and her brother Rahul ate 2/4 of the same pizza. How much pizza did they eat altogether?

Solution:

Reena ate 1/4 of the pizza.

Rahul ate 2/4 of the pizza.

Total pizza eaten = 1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4

They ate 3/4 of the pizza altogether.

Problem 2:

Aarav had 1/2 metre of ribbon. He used 1/4 metre to make a bookmark. How much ribbon does he have left?

Solution:

Aarav had 1/2 metre of ribbon.

He used 1/4 metre for the bookmark.

Ribbon left = 1/2 - 1/4

To subtract, we need the same denominators.

1/2 = 2/4

So, 2/4 - 1/4 = 1/4

Aarav has 1/4 metre of ribbon left.

Practice Questions

  1. What fraction of a day is 6 hours?
  2. If you divide a chocolate bar into 8 equal pieces and eat 3 pieces, what fraction of the chocolate did you eat?
  3. Maya has read 2/5 of her book, and Sanya has read 1/5 of the same book. Who has read more and by what fraction?
  4. Write 3/8 and 5/8 in order from smallest to largest.
  5. Find the sum: 1/6 + 2/6 + 3/6
